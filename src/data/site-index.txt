Smile-fitting models. The jump page at aigamma.com/jump hosts the canonical smile-fitting model lineage as five reading surfaces calibrated against the same SPX expiration slice: Variance Gamma as a pure-jump infinite-activity Levy process with no diffusive component; Heston with CIR variance and the leverage correlation rho as the no-jumps stochastic-vol benchmark; Bates SVJ with eight-parameter calibration combining Heston variance and Merton jumps; Kou with asymmetric double-exponential jumps; and Merton with log-normal Poisson jumps as the historical anchor. The page opens with the most extreme departures from BSM (pure jumps, then pure stoch vol) and walks through the conventional jump-augmented diffusions, with a dashed Heston-only counterfactual on the Bates slot that lets the reader visualize directly what the jump component contributes on top of the Heston piece. The /smile/ URL 301-redirects to /jump/ for any reader still using a bookmarked link from when the multi-model smile read was its own page; the /stochastic/ URL 301-redirects to /jump/ for the same reason.
